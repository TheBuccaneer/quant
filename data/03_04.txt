### So kannst du das im Paper berichten (kurz + sauber)

**Method / Reliability (CTClass):**

> “Two raters initially labeled CTClass independently and achieved about 67% agreement. After discussing disagreements, we refined the codebook by introducing the B-subclasses (B1/B2), which increased agreement to about 83% on a follow-up labeling round. Remaining borderline cases were resolved using a conservative decision rule to avoid overestimating compile-time checkability: when uncertain, we favored B2 over B1, and C over B2. Final labels were obtained by consensus/adjudication.”

### Wichtige Klarstellung (damit Reviewer nicht meckern)

* Die **67%** und **83%** sind **Pre-Adjudication** Werte (also *vor* finaler Einigung).
* Alles **danach** ist “adjudicated consensus” und zählt nicht mehr als unabhängige Übereinstimmung, sondern als **Gold-Label-Erstellung**.

Wenn du nur Percent Agreement hast, ist das okay. Eleganter wäre zusätzlich κ, aber nicht zwingend, wenn du die Zahlen transparent einordnest.

### Warum die konservative Regel gut ist (und was du dazu sagen solltest)

Dein Tie-break “B1→B2→C” ist methodisch sinnvoll, weil er:

* das Risiko reduziert, **CT-Checkbarkeit zu überschätzen** (also nicht zu optimistisch zu labeln),
* aber zugleich systematisch die Verteilung Richtung **weniger “checkbar”** verschiebt.

Ein Satz reicht:

> “This conservative tie-break may bias labels toward lower checkability; we chose it intentionally to avoid optimistic estimates.”

Textbaustein fürs Paper (Reliability + Threat)

Labeling reliability. Two raters independently coded the CTClass dimension. The initial agreement was approximately 67%. After discussing disagreements, we refined the codebook by introducing the B-subclasses (B1/B2), which increased agreement to approximately 83% in a second round. Remaining borderline cases were resolved by adjudication using a conservative tie-break rule to avoid overestimating compile-time checkability (prefer B2 over B1, and C over B2). The final dataset therefore represents consensus labels derived from this procedure.
Threats to validity. Since the conservative tie-break systematically favors lower checkability, our CTClass distribution may be biased toward B2/C; we intentionally accepted this bias to avoid optimistic conclusions. Further, only CTClass was double-coded; other dimensions (e.g., BugType, StackLayer) may contain higher subjective variance and should be interpreted descriptively.
